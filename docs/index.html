Install on Android

Sideloading a test build takes about a minute.

  1. Download the APK using the button above, on the Android phone itself.
  2. Open the downloaded file (from the notification or your Files / Downloads app).
  3. Allow installs from this source when prompted — Android asks the first time you install outside the Play Store. Tap Settings → Allow from this source, then go back.
  4. Tap Install, then Open. That’s it.
Heads up: this is an unsigned test build for trying the app before it reaches the Play Store. Google Play Protect may show a generic “unknown app” warning — choose Install anyway. The release Play Store build will be properly signed.

Try it out

A quick run-through to test the full reporting flow.

  • Tap Report a sighting on the home screen.
  • Pick the species — try Finlayson’s squirrel and read the ID hint.
  • Add a photo with the camera or from your gallery.
  • Tap Use my location, or tap the map to place the pin yourself.
  • Answer the quick details (how many, what it was doing, where).
  • Optionally add your contact details — or leave them blank to stay anonymous.
  • Tap Send report to DENR — your email app opens, pre-filled and addressed to bmb@bmb.gov.ph with the photo attached. Send it (or just check the draft while testing).
  • Open My reports to see it saved with its status. Try airplane mode to see offline behaviour.
There is no server behind this app. Reports are delivered straight to the agency from your device, and your history is kept locally on the phone.

What’s inside

Built to be rebranded for any animal, country, and agency.

📸 Photo & evidence

Capture or attach a photo so the agency can confirm the species.

📍 Map pin & GPS

Auto-locate or tap an OpenStreetMap to mark exactly where you saw it.

✈️ Works offline

Reports save on the device and send when you’re back online.

🔁 Reusable engine

Swap the campaign config to report any species to any agency, by email or API.
